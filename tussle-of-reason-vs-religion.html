The Tussle of Reason vs Religion: Searching answer for existence of God


Stephen William Hawking (8 January 1942 – 14 March 2018) was an English theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author who was director of research at the Centre for Theoretical Cosmology at the University of Cambridge at the time of his death. He was the Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at the University of Cambridge between 1979 and 2009. He is considered as one of the most brilliant minds in the history of mankind. As reported by Jamie Ducharme in an article on March 14, 2018 in Times

"I regard the brain as a computer which will stop working when its components fail," he (Stephen Hawking) told the Guardian. "There is no heaven or afterlife for broken down computers; that is a fairy story for people afraid of the dark.""God is the name people give to the reason we are here," he (Stephen Hawking) said. "But I think that reason is the laws of physics rather than someone with whom one can have a personal relationship. An impersonal God."

In his book "Brief Answers to Big Questions" Stephen Hawking writes at different spots that

"I think the universe was spontaneously created out of nothing, according to the laws of science"
"If you accept, as I do, that the laws of nature are fixed, then it doesn't take long to ask: What role is there for God?"
"If you like, you can say the laws are the work of God, but that is more a definition of God than a proof of his existence"
"Did God create the quantum laws that allowed the Big Bang to occur? I have no desire to offend anyone of faith, but I think science has a more compelling explanation than a divine creator."
"The universe itself, in all its mind-boggling vastness and complexity, could simply have popped into existence without violating the known laws of nature"
""there was no time before the Big Bang."
"We have Finally found something that doesn’t have a cause, because there was no time for a cause to exist in, for me this means that there is no possibility of a creator, because there is no time for a creator to have existed in."
"We have this one life to appreciate the grand design of the universe, and for that I am extremely grateful."


Stephen Hawking, without any doubt, was a great scientist to whom science and humans owe a lot. But why Stephen never believed in the existence of a Creator? Wasn’t he smart enough or was he smart enough? If a man with that much knowledge and vision did not believed in a Creator, then how a man with simple mind can be convinced to believe in a Creator of this universe? So why the Prophet PBUH directed to seek refuge in ALLAH when asked such questions. Let’s start with a simple example. One day I was sitting with my grandfather watching a documentary about Neil Armstrong’s landing on the moon. My grandfather could not understand what it was so he asked me what were they telling in the documentary. I answered in layman’s wording that they are telling how a man landed on the moon. He gave a laughter and said in Punjabi "What type foolish idea are you listening. How is it possible that a man can travel to moon? They are just making you fool." I tried to convince him with best of my knowledge but he stuck to his point, so finally I gave up. The reason behind why I was not able to convince him was that there was a large gap between his knowledge of science and contemporary developments of science. Move just 2 centuries back in time and consider yourself telling a person writing a letter to his friend in another city that "Why are you writing a letter to your friend. Why can’t you just WhatsApp him?" It might sound funny but you would end up in a mental hospital either while trying to convince him or due to being considered already insane. Now move a bit back and give same advice to a person living in civilization of Greeks, Romans, Mesopotamia, Egypt or Indus. You can’t convince about existence of WhatsApp (which a 4-year-old child uses today) to Aristotle or Socrates which were considered most brilliant minds of their time. Just by giving a gap of around 2000-2500 years, you can’t explain the simplest phenomenon of today’s world to the most brilliant minds of history. It is because we are 2000 years ahead of time, so how can we be convinced with mere logic or knowledge that ALLAH has such and such brilliant attributes. This is where believe steps in. Now consider that Aristotle was somehow brought in today’s world and he was shown and given the "privilege" to use WhatsApp and send him back to his time. When he would tell his students about this "unbelievable" invention, some of his students would call Aristotle either lair or either insane. But there would be some students who would believe that a technology like WhatsApp is possible, not on the basis of logic or science but on the basis of believe which they would have in Aristotle. Aristotle might spend his entire life his students convincing them just on the basis of logic, but would fail. Same students would be convinced immediately, if they believed that Aristotle is speaking truth. So, some phenomenon need believe a lot more than evidence to be convinced. This discussion became the main subject of the research. Primary problem while this research was that black hole were invisible and could only be detected or felt on the basis of their gravitational effect on their surroundings. Practically Black Holes were invisible but their existence was strongly believed. On 10 April,2019 first ever image of a Black Hole was released which was captured by Event Horizon Telescope (EHT) after 2 years of data collection. Black Hole was still invisible in this picture. It was recognized by gases at the edge of the Black Holes before getting trapped into Black Hole. "We are immediately aware of the three dimensions that surround us on a daily basis – those that define the length, width, and depth of all objects in our universes (the x, y, and z axes, respectively). In fact, the theoretical framework of Superstring Theory posits that the universe exists in ten different dimensions. He states 10 dimensions of universe as
  1. Length
  2. Width
  3. Depth/Height
  4. Time
  5. In fifth dimension, we would see a world slightly different from our own that would give us a means of measuring the similarity and differences between our world and other possible ones.
  6. In the sixth, we would see a plane of possible worlds, where we could compare and position all the possible universes that start with the same initial conditions as this one.
  7. In seventh dimension, you have access to the possible worlds that start with different initial conditions.
  8. The eighth dimension again gives us a plane of such possible universe histories, each of which begins with different initial conditions and branches out infinitely (hence why they are called infinities).
  9. In the ninth dimension, we can compare all the possible universe histories, starting with all the different possible laws of physics and initial conditions.
  10. In the tenth and final dimension, we arrive at the point in which everything possible and imaginable is covered.
